What counts as a real AI automation, and what's just a demo

Maria Jose Pava · Director of AI Solutions and Delivery · June 23, 2026
AI Automations

A real AI automation runs without anyone babysitting it: a trigger happens, the system acts, and a person only gets involved when something needs a judgment call. A demo is everything that looks impressive in a meeting and then never gets installed. The difference matters more than which AI tool you pick.

Trigger, action, judgment call: the three parts of every automation

Every real automation has the same basic shape. Something triggers it, a form submission, a new record, a scheduled time, a message coming in. The system takes an action based on that trigger, enriching data, sending a message, updating a record, generating content. And somewhere, a line gets drawn for when a human needs to step in instead of the system guessing. Skip any one of those three parts and what you have is a script, not an automation your team can rely on.

What separates a one-step build from a multi-system build

Not every automation needs to be complicated to be useful. A single trigger and a single action inside one tool, a new lead getting tagged and routed in HubSpot, for example, is a complete, working automation on its own. A multi-system build that pulls data from three different tools, runs it through scoring logic, and triggers actions across two more systems is a different scope entirely, with a different price and timeline. We break this down into four tiers on our pricing page so it's clear what you're actually paying for before you commit to anything.

Why the first one should be free

The easiest way to tell a real automation from a sales pitch is to see one actually running. That's why we build your first automation for free, install it with you, and let you watch it work before any money changes hands. If it's useful, we talk about what's next. If it's not, you keep it anyway.

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take to build a basic automation?

A single trigger-and-action build typically takes a few days from the scoping call to installation. More complex, multi-system builds take longer depending on how many tools are involved.

Do I need a CRM already in place?

No. Plenty of the automations we build are what gets a team's data into a CRM in the first place. If you don't have one yet, that's a conversation worth having on the first call.

Which AI tool should we use, Claude, ChatGPT, or something else?

Whichever one actually fits the job and the systems you're already using. We're not loyal to one vendor. Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Zapier, and HubSpot all show up in our builds depending on what the work requires.
