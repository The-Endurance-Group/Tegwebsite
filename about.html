About
"Through endurance we conquer." - Ernest Shackleton

We've been doing this work since 1998. The tools have changed. The principle hasn't.

The Endurance Group was founded by Jim Sullivan, named for Shackleton's ship and the standard it set: stay steady when things get hard, and finish what you start.

For more than two decades, Jim worked directly inside B2B companies - professional services firms, real estate teams, growing software companies - building the relationships that drove their growth. Along the way, one thing became obvious: those companies didn't fail because they lacked good ideas. They stalled because nobody had the time or the system to act on those ideas consistently.

Tools got bought and never used. Good intentions got lost in busy weeks. Momentum slipped, not from lack of ambition, but because no one was watching the follow-through.

So we evolved.

Today

Today, The Endurance Group is an AI automation firm. Jim and his son Conor Sullivan lead a team that builds custom AI automations for B2B companies - professional services firms, software and tech companies, and real estate teams - and stays on as a partner to keep those automations running and improving. Jim brings 25 years of experience knowing which work actually moves a business forward. Conor brings the technical layer: AI implementation, automation architecture, and the discipline to build things that run without anyone babysitting them.

We're not a piece of software you buy once, and we're not a consultant who hands you a slide deck and leaves. We're a partner who builds with you, stays with you, and keeps finding the next thing worth automating.

What makes us different

Most firms give you one or the other: strategy with no execution, or execution with no strategy. We give you both, and we don't disappear after the first build.

Strategy and execution, together

We don't just build one automation and disappear. We're your strategic partner, building and managing your automation portfolio alongside you and growing it over time.

Warm paths, not cold outreach

When outreach is part of the work, we map social capital and find warm paths into prospects through shared schools, past employers, and mutual connections. It's why our response rates look the way they do.

A portfolio that grows with you
