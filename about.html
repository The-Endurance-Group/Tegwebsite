About
"Through endurance we conquer." — Ernest Shackleton

We've been doing this work since 1998. The tools have changed. The principle hasn't.

The Endurance Group was founded by Jim Sullivan, named for Shackleton's ship and the standard it set: stay steady when things get hard, and finish what you start.

We began as an outsourced sales and marketing partner for professional services firms. Over more than two decades, one thing became obvious: growth doesn't fail because companies lack ideas. It fails because execution breaks down.

Sales teams chase the wrong accounts. Marketing supports activity instead of conversations. Tools get bought and never used. Momentum stalls, not from lack of ambition, but because no one is accountable for outcomes.

So we evolved.

Today

Jim and his son Conor Sullivan now lead the work together. Jim brings over 25 years of sales execution, relationship strategy, and the insight that most B2B revenue comes from who you know and how you activate that network. Conor brings the technical layer, AI implementation, automation architecture, product development, and the ability to build things that actually run without a person babysitting them.

Together they run a business that does both: the human execution and the AI systems that make it scale.

What makes us different

Most firms give you one or the other. A strategy with no one to execute it. A tool with no one to run it. We sit at the intersection of real sales execution and real AI implementation, and we stay in the work until the pipeline is moving.

We also build our own technology. Commonality is a proprietary tool we built to map social capital, find warm paths into prospects through shared schools, past employers, and mutual connections, and use that context to draft outreach that opens with something real. It powers the outreach work we do for clients and it's why our response rates look the way they do.

We start with the work. Then we build what makes execution consistent and revenue predictable.

If you want a partner who executes, not just advises, we should talk.
